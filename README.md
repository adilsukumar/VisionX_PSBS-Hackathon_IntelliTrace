## ‚ö†Ô∏è The Problem: Undetected Fraud 
In supply chain finance, a single compromised supplier can trigger a chain reaction. For example, a single Tier 1 supplier fabricated 340 fraudulent invoices, all of which bypassed traditional validation checks undetected.

**Why Existing Systems Fail:**
* **Siloed Lender Systems:** Each lender operates in isolation, with no shared visibility across institutions, making cross-entity fraud patterns completely invisible.
* **Reliance on Simple Format Checks:** Current systems only validate invoice format and GST compliance, missing deeper anomalies like duplicate submissions or fabricated vendor identities.
* **No Cross-Tier Correlation:** Existing tools cannot connect risk signals across Tier 1, 2, and 3 suppliers, leaving dangerous exposure undetected before disbursement occurs.
* **Reactive Detection:** Fraud is typically discovered only after funds are disbursed, making recovery difficult and financial damage to lenders nearly unavoidable.
* **Invisible Collusion Networks:** Shell companies and colluding entities exploit siloed data structures, operating undetected because no system maps their hidden relational connections.
* **Static Risk Models:** Legacy risk assessments are snapshots, not dynamic, and fail to adapt to evolving fraud patterns or real-time changes in supply chain behavior.

## üí° Our Solution: IntelliTrace

## üß† Multi-Factor Risk Scoring Engine 
Our Risk Engine fuses multiple factors into a single, real-time Composite Risk Rating (e.g., 8.7/10) for instant approve, review, or block decisions:

* **Invoice Fingerprint Score:** Uses unique cryptographic hashes to detect exact and near-duplicate invoices instantly, blocking resubmission fraud.
* **Revenue Mismatch Index:** AI compares declared revenues against transaction volumes to flag statistical outliers.
* **Velocity Score:** Tracks submission frequency and transaction speed, identifying abnormal spikes indicating coordinated fraud bursts.
* **Graph Anomaly Weight:** Measures suspicious clustering, hidden shell-company links, and collusion patterns within the live graph.
* **Exposure Amplification Factor:** Quantifies cascading risk by calculating how one flagged node amplifies financial exposure across connections.
